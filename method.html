Injection-induced seismicity in the Permian Basin constrains saltwater disposal (SWD), enhanced oil recovery (EOR), and carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) operations. Existing reactive traffic-light systems often lead to unnecessary shutdowns, reducing energy production and slowing the deployment of low-carbon technologies. In this study, a Transformer-based model is developed to predict induced seismicity over the next 60 days, enabling proactive injection control through a Reinforcement Learning (RL) agent. By implementing a reward–penalty mechanism, the RL framework optimizes injection strategies based on spatial seismic risk indicators. Specifically, areas with low seismic risk (characterized by high b-values) are assigned higher injection rates, while areas with elevated seismic risk (low b-values) are subjected to reduced injection. Our results demonstrate that this approach can effectively anticipate seismic hazards. In particular, up to 10 days prior to Mw > 4 events, the model identifies zones with decreasing b-values, allowing the RL agent to adjust injection accordingly. The optimized strategy 30% increases injection in low-risk regions (high b-value zones), while reducing injection by up to 30% in high-risk regions (low b-value zones). This adaptive control maintains operational efficiency while effectively mitigating seismic risk.
