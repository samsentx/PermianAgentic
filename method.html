Transformer-Based Forecasting Engine

The forecasting engine of PermianAgentic is built upon a Transformer Time Series architecture specifically designed to model complex temporal dynamics in subsurface systems. The model utilizes the scaled dot-product attention mechanism, enabling it to learn relationships across all observations within a sequence rather than relying solely on adjacent time steps. This approach allows the system to capture long-range dependencies and evolving temporal patterns while avoiding the information loss commonly encountered in traditional recurrent neural networks (Vaswani et al., 2017; Li et al., 2023). The architecture follows an encoder–decoder framework. The encoder transforms historical operational and seismic observations into contextual representations, while the decoder leverages these learned representations to generate future forecasts. Multi-head attention layers allow the model to simultaneously learn multiple representations of the data, improving its ability to identify hidden patterns, nonlinear relationships, and emerging trends within complex time-series datasets. To preserve temporal order, positional encoding is incorporated into the input embeddings. This enables the model to recognize sequential structure while maintaining the flexibility of attention-based learning. The forecasting framework is implemented in Python using TensorFlow and executed within a Google Colab environment.

Seismic Hazard Intelligence

PermianAgentic combines advanced forecasting with spatial seismic risk assessment to provide actionable intelligence for injection management. Transformer-based earthquake forecasting models continuously analyze historical seismicity and operational data to identify potential increases in seismic hazard. The system is capable of detecting elevated risk conditions up to ten days before the occurrence of potential Mw > 4 induced seismic events. In parallel, spatial b-value analysis based on the Gutenberg–Richter relationship is performed to characterize stress conditions throughout the basin. Areas exhibiting low b-values are interpreted as regions of elevated seismic susceptibility, whereas high b-value regions generally indicate lower seismic risk. These dynamic risk maps provide a continuously updated representation of basin-wide seismic conditions.

Agentic AI Decision Framework

Beyond forecasting and risk assessment, PermianAgentic incorporates an Agentic AI framework that enables proactive and autonomous SWD injection management. The platform integrates Transformer-based forecasting, spatial b-value intelligence, and Reinforcement Learning (RL) into a closed-loop decision-making architecture. The RL agent continuously evaluates evolving seismic and operational conditions and determines optimal injection strategies across the basin. When elevated seismic risk is identified, injection volumes can be reduced in high-risk regions characterized by low b-values. Conversely, injection capacity can be strategically increased in lower-risk regions characterized by higher b-values. This adaptive redistribution of injection activity helps maintain operational efficiency while reducing the likelihood of induced seismic events.

Autonomous SWD Control

The decision engine operates through a reward–penalty optimization framework that balances two critical objectives: maximizing operational performance and minimizing seismic risk. As new production, injection, and seismic observations become available, the system automatically updates forecasts, reassesses spatial risk, and refines operational recommendations. This closed-loop architecture transforms traditional reactive injection management into a proactive, data-driven control strategy. By continuously learning from new information, PermianAgentic provides dynamic basin-scale intelligence that supports safer, more efficient, and more sustainable SWD operations.
