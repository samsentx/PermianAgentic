In this platform, a Transformer Time Series-based deep learning architecture is employed for time-series forecasting. The model is built upon the scaled dot-product attention mechanism, which enables the model to learn dependencies across all time steps in the input sequence (Vaswani et al., 2017; Li et al., 2023). This capability allows it to effectively capture long-range temporal relationships while mitigating the vanishing information problem commonly observed in traditional recurrent models. The architecture follows an encoder–decoder structure. The encoder processes the input time series to generate contextual representations, while the decoder utilizes these representations along with previously generated outputs to produce future predictions. Both encoder and decoder are composed of stacked multi-head attention layers and position-wise feed-forward neural networks. Since the Transformer lacks an inherent notion of sequence order, positional encoding is incorporated into the input embeddings to preserve temporal information. Furthermore, multi-head attention allows the model to learn multiple representation subspaces in parallel, enhancing its ability to capture complex patterns in time-series data. The model is implemented in Python using TensorFlow within a Jupyter Notebook environment on Google Colab. In addition to forecasting, An Agentic AI framework was developed to enable proactive SWD injection control in the Permian Basin. The system integrates a Transformer-based earthquake forecasting model (Vaswani et al., 2017; Li et al., 2023), spatial b-value analysis based on the Gutenberg–Richter relationship (Gutenberg and Richter, 1944; Aki, 1965), and a Reinforcement Learning (RL) agent (Sutton and Barto, 2018) within a closed-loop decision-making architecture. The forecasting model identifies areas of increasing seismic hazard up to 10 days before potential Mw > 4 events, while b-value maps provide spatial risk characterization. Based on these risk indicators, the RL agent autonomously optimizes injection strategies by increasing injection rates in low-risk regions (high b-values) and reducing injection rates in high-risk regions (low b-values). Through a reward–penalty mechanism that balances operational efficiency and seismic risk mitigation, the Agentic AI system continuously adapts its decisions as new seismic and operational data become available, enabling dynamic and proactive management of induced seismicity.
